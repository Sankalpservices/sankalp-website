Frequently Asked Questions

What is the minimum number of members required to form a housing society?

+

According to Section 6 of MCS Act 1960, a minimum of 10 adult persons is required to form a cooperative housing society. These persons must be competent to contract under the Indian Contract Act.

How often should Annual General Meetings be conducted?

+

As per Section 75 of MCS Act, every society must hold an Annual General Meeting within 6 months of the close of the cooperative year. The meeting must be held within the state and proper notice must be given to all members.

What are the audit requirements for cooperative societies?

+

Under Section 81, every society must have its accounts audited annually by qualified auditors. The audit should be completed within 6 months of the cooperative year end, and the audit report must be presented at the AGM.

Can a committee member be removed during their tenure?

+

Yes, according to Section 73-ID, a no-confidence motion can be passed against officers. Also, under Section 78A, the Registrar has powers to remove committee members for misconduct or incapacity.

What percentage of shares can a single member hold?

+

As per Section 28, no member can hold more than 1/5th (20%) of the total share capital of the society, or shares worth more than Rs. 1,000, whichever is higher, unless specifically allowed by the bylaws.

What is the reserve fund requirement for societies?

+

Under Section 66, every society must transfer at least 25% of its net profits to the reserve fund until it equals the share capital or such amount as prescribed by the Registrar.
